A Letter from the Secretary General





Greetings!


I, Sarthak Bansal, on behalf of the Secretariat, am honored to cordially invite you to the second iteration of the CHIREC Junior Model United Nations, or the CHIREC JMUN 2018.

This iteration of the conference aims to provide a platform directed at students of grades 6 to 8 to convey their unique views through debate and discussion on increasingly prominent, constantly changing world issues. We aim to provide a stimulating environment where amateurs and experienced delegates alike can collaborate at a level that promotes growth and learning, research and articulation alone with essential qualities such as diplomacy, bureaucracy and other skills required for success in today’s world, where the aforementioned skills are becoming increasingly important.

The Chirec JMUN aims to be a revolutionary instrument in making the Hyderabad MUN Circuit more accessible and open for junior delegates. The first edition left a memorable impression on both the attendees and organizers, and we promise a remarkable and memorable experience this year as well, with the help of our impeccable, hard-working Secretariat and dedicated Organizing Committee.

We hope you attend the conference, ameliorate your skills, and immerse yourself into one of the best MUNs the city of Hyderabad has to offer.


Sarthak Bansal,
Secretary-General,

Being one of the six principal organs of the United Nations , the Security Council is the United Nations crisis – management body empowered to impose binding obligations on the 193 member states to maintain world peace and security. It’s powers include the establishment of peace-keeping operations , the establishment of international sanctions , and the authorization of military actions through SC resolutions. The UNSC has proved itself to be one of the most controversial and highly advanced committees ; demanding intense research and knowledge. The Social, Humanitarian and Cultural Committee is the third General Assembly of the United Nations. It is tasked with a broad mandate consisting of social and humanitarian affairs as well as human rights issues affecting the world population. Other than examining human rights questions, SOCHUM discusses questions related to the protection of children, the treatment of refugees, elimination of racism and discrimination and the advancement of women. Human rights are not optional, and to deny people their rights is to challenge their very humanity, so buckle up for some intense debate, it can’t get any better!

“I want to die on Mars. Just not on impact”


The United Nations Office for Outer Space Affairs (UNOOSA) is the United Nations office responsible for promoting international cooperation in the peaceful uses of outer space. UNOOSA conducts international workshops, training courses and pilot projects on topics that include remote sensing, satellite navigation, satellite meteorology, tele-education and basic space sciences for the benefit of developing nations. UNOOSA also prepares and distributes reports, studies and publications on various fields of space science and technology applications and international space law. “What the two hands of the labourer can achieve, the capitalist will never get with gold and silver”
International Labour Organization (ILO) was established in order to promote international and lasting peace through social justice as the first specialized agency that was incorporated into the United Nations. The ILO primarily aims to set international labour standards, promote rights at work, support decent employment opportunities, and enrich social protection through strengthening of dialogue on issues of work. It also focuses on providing equal opportunities for both women and men for obtaining decent work concerning freedom, security, equity, and human dignity. We’re expecting great things here because after all, a labour organization is not a debating club, it is a place of action.

DISEC

Where will the conference be held?

The conference will take place at the main campus of CHIREC International School in Kondapur. The address is displayed on the website.

When is the conference?

The conference will be held on the 2nd, 3rd and 4th of November (on Friday, Saturday and Sunday).



What is the dress code?

The dress code for the CHIREC JMUN consists of strictly western formals, all 3 days. Girls must not wear dresses or skirts, even if they fall below the knees. Failure to adhere to the code will result in expulsion from the conference.



How can the delegate learn about the proceedings of the MUN?

All allocated delegates will be provided a delegate handbook regarding the rules of the procedure and the proceeding of the MUN.



Can I bring electronic devices?

Yes, you may bring up to 2 devices for research purposes only, one phone and one laptop or tablet. International Press (IP) members must bring their own cameras. These must be registered through the IT forms. Please be informed that you are not allowed to take any pictures on campus, and only the IP is allowed to do so.



Will I have to pay a fee to register for the JMUN?

Yes, if you are allocated in a committee, you will have to pay a certain amount of money to be able to attend the conference. The details for payment will be intimated to those who have been allocated. The fees for various rounds are different, so apply as soon as possible to get your preferred allocation at a reduced price!



Is lunch included with the delegate fees?

The school will provide all those attending with lunch at the school cafeteria. However, if you wish to bring extra money, you can do so, as we have many stalls where you can purchase snacks and other fast food! In case of any food allergies, please let us know so that we can make appropriate changes.



Will there be transportation provided?

We do not provide any transportation to or from the venue. Your school may arrange for transport, but if not, you will have to avail your own transport for the 3 days.



Will there be a training session for delegates?

We will be having online sessions for delegates who wish to know how an MUN works.
